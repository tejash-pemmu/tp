Pollution

Pollution is the introduction of contaminants into the natural environment that cause harm.Pollution can take the form of any substance or energy.Pollutants, the components of pollution, can be either foreign substances/energies or naturally occurring contaminants.The five main types of pollution are:Air, Water, Soil, Noise, and Light pollution.These forms of pollution can have detrimental effects on the environment, human health, and ecosystems.

Air Pollution

Air pollution is contamination of the indoor or outdoor environment by any chemical, physical or biological agent that modifies the natural characteristics of the atmosphere.

Water Pollution

Water pollution is the release of substances into bodies of water that make water unsafe for human use and disrupt aquatic ecosystems.

Soil Pollution

Soil pollution is defined as contamination of soil at higher than normal concentrations by waste materials of human origin that have adverse effects on human and ecosystem health.

Noise Pollution

Noise pollution is the propagation of noise or sound with potential harmful effects on humans and animals.The source of outdoor noise worldwide is mainly caused by machines, transport and propagation systems.

Light Pollution

Light pollution is the excessive or misdirected use of artificial light outdoors, which can negatively impact wildlife, human health, and the environment.
